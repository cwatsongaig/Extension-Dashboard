
Purpose
Translate underwriting policy for financial statement requirements and Account Review Report (ARR) frequency into clear, testable application requirements.


In-Scope Definitions

ARR (Account Review Report): A review event triggered by receipt of financial statements.
ARR Frequency: Required cadence for completing ARRs (Annual, Semi-Annual, Quarterly).
Financial Statement Event: Receipt of annual CPA statements or interim statements.
Authority Level: Branch, Director-level, or beyond Director-level.


Business Rules (Authoritative)
BR-1: ARR Trigger

An ARR shall be required upon receipt of a financial statement (annual CPA or interim).
BR-2: Minimum Financial Statement Expectations (Active Accounts)

Active accounts shall require: One annual CPA financial statement, and
At least one interim financial statement per year (preferred mid-year).
BR-3: ARR Completion Timeline

Once a financial statement is received, the ARR shall be completed within 14 calendar days.
BR-4: ARR Frequency by Authority Level

Branch-level accounts: Minimum: Annual ARR.
Exception: Annual-only ARR requires Director approval and is intended for low-risk accounts.
Director-level accounts: At least two ARRs per year (Annual + one additional interim-driven ARR).
Beyond Director-level accounts: Quarterly ARRs (four per year) unless explicitly reduced by the highest supervisor.
BR-5: Supervisor Overrides

The highest supervisor may reduce ARR frequency below quarterly for beyond Director-level accounts.
All overrides must be recorded with approver identity, date, and rationale.
BR-6: No-Delay Principle

ARR completion must not be blocked by missing supplemental documents once a financial statement is received.


Functional Requirements
FR-1: Financial Statement Intake

The system shall capture financial statement events with: Statement type (Annual CPA / Interim)
Statement period end date
Date received
Source (Agent, Principal, System Upload)
FR-2: ARR Requirement Evaluation

Upon each Financial Statement Event, the system shall evaluate whether an ARR is required based on ARR Frequency rules.
FR-3: ARR Due Date Calculation

The system shall automatically set ARR Due Date = Financial Statement Date Received + 14 days.
FR-4: ARR Frequency Enforcement

The system shall enforce the minimum ARR frequency based on Authority Level unless an approved override exists.
FR-5: Override Management

The system shall allow supervisors to: Approve reduced ARR frequency
Record justification notes
Define override effective period
FR-6: Notifications & Alerts

The system shall generate alerts: When an ARR is required
When an ARR is 7 days from due
When an ARR is overdue
FR-7: Completion Recording

The system shall record ARR completion with: Completion date
Responsible underwriter
Review level achieved
FR-8: Audit Trail

The system shall maintain a complete audit history for: Financial statement receipt
ARR creation, updates, completion
Frequency overrides

Decision Logic (Rules Engine Friendly)
ON FinancialStatementReceived:
  IF Account.status != Active:
    EXIT

  requiredFrequency = determineFrequency(Account.authorityLevel, Override)

  IF ARRNeededBasedOnFrequency(requiredFrequency):
    CREATE ARR
    SET ARR.dueDate = statement.receivedDate + 14 days




Validation Rules

ARR cannot be marked complete without a triggering Financial Statement.
ARR Frequency cannot be reduced without supervisor override.
Override must expire or be actively renewed.


Non-Functional Requirements

Auditability: All decisions must be explainable for audit review.
Configurability: Authority thresholds and frequencies must be configurable without code deployment.
Performance: ARR evaluation must occur in near-real-time upon statement receipt.
Security: Override permissions restricted to supervisory roles.


Reporting Requirements

Accounts missing required financial statements
Upcoming and overdue ARRs
Accounts operating under overridden frequencies
Trend of ARR completion timeliness
